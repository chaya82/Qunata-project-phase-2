Dear Sir/Madame,

You are cordially invited to attend the 3rd Workshop on Sustainable Management of Plastic to Leverage Circular Economy and Achieve SDG in Bangladesh which will be held through webinar on July 22, 2020 at 10.00am – 1.00pm Dhaka time. The workshop will be organized by the World Bank in association with the Department of Environment (DoE) and Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC).

In February 2019, the MoEFCC, DoE, supported by the World Bank and other partners organized the event on Sustainable Management of Plastics to Leverage Circular Economy and Achieve SDGs in Bangladesh. The second event held in September 2019 focused on the discussion on policy reform, especially on Extended Producer Responsibility and designed its pilot together with stakeholders. The third event will launch innovation contest calling for solutions that fit to EPR scheme currently being discussed with the stakeholders in Bangladesh. It will also include consultation of the World Bank funded Plastic Free Rivers and Seas for South Asia Regional Project which provides grant support for innovations.

To access the event, registration is required at the following link, after registering you will be able to join the event page 15 minutes prior to the webinar.

SPEAKERS

 
Mohammad Anis, Acting Country Director, The World Bank

Anis is a development strategist and practitioner in Power and Energy sector whose 22 years of hands on experiences cover contributions in both public and private sector entities in Bangladesh and Afghanistan. Anis joined World Bank in 2008 and led preparation and implementation of several large and complex operations in energy infrastructure as well as climate change & disaster risk management. In the power and energy sector, his work exposures include power generation, transmission & distribution, renewable energy, energy efficiency and regional integration. He also made key contributions to the transformation of state-owned enterprises in Bangladesh power sector. As cross-support to other units within World Bank, Anis has also managed projects involving construction and improvement of disaster shelters, repair of coastal embankment and restoration of agricultural livelihoods affected by the cyclones in 2007 and 2009 in Bangladesh. Anis holds an MBA in Finance and a bachelor’s degree in Mechanical Engineering.
 
 
 
Mr. Cyrill Gutsch, CEO, Parley for the Oceans

Cyrill is an award-winning designer and brand/product developer. In 1998 he created a method called Cross Intelligence, which brings a culture of collaboration to major organizations. In 2012 he decided to focus on a new client vital to us all: the oceans. He founded Parley for the Oceans as a collaboration network for creators, thinkers and leaders to raise awareness for the beauty and fragility of the oceans and to develop and implement strategies that can end their destruction. Parley and their partners won the Grand Prix for Product Design at Cannes Lions, two D&AD Pencils and an ADCN Grand Prix in 2014. Gutsch was named 2017 Environmentalist of the Year by the Surf Industry Manufacturers Association for his tireless work for the oceans. In 2018, Parley was named Environmental Organisation of the Year at EARTHx and Gutsch was honored with a Special Recognition Award for Innovation by the British Fashion Council. In 2019, Gutsch was inducted into the UBS Global Visionary community – a select group of leading entrepreneurs and individuals working towards one or more of the 17 UN Sustainable Development Goals.
 
 
 
Mr. W. K. Rathnadeera, Senior Programme Officer, South Asia Co-operative Environment Programme

Rathnadeera is a Senior Programme Officer of South Asia Co-operative Environment Programme (SACEP). SACEP is an intergovernmental Organization established by eight South Asian countries. He is a Master Degree holder in Environmental Science from the Chiang Mai University, Thailand in 1998. He joined SACEP in 2003 and since then he is involving in preparing and managing a number of projects implemented by SACEP in South Asia. Currently he is involving in preparing the “Plastic Free Rivers and Seas for South Asia Project” founded by the World Bank and Parley. Before Joining SACEP, he worked in Ministry of Environment, Sri Lanka.
 
 
 
Ms. Suiko Yoshijima, Senior Environmental Specialist, The World Bank

Suiko is a Senior Environmental Specialist at the World Bank's Bangkok office. She currently manages several pollution management projects in Bangladesh. She has extensive experience working on industrial pollution, urban pollution, chemical management, marine pollution and non-hazardous/hazardous waste management. Suiko has various publications including Country Environmental Analysis in Bangladesh, Resource Efficiency and Cleaner Production, and Air Pollution in Greater Cairo. She obtained her Master in Environmental Studies from the University of Tokyo and a Master in Public Administration from Harvard's Kennedy School.
 
 
 
Ms. Nivedha Raghavan, Founder & CEO, TrashCon Labs Private Limited

Nivedha RM is an accomplished Entrepreneur who is a big supporter of segregation and has invented world’s first completely automated mixed municipal waste segregation unit. Her invention has been recognized globally by the World Bank, Red Cross, UNDP, Israel Embassy and has received several coveted awards nationally as well as Internationally. It is now successfully implemented across Gujarat and Bengaluru and has ongoing projects in 4 other states. They have recently signed up with Airport Authority of India and are carrying out a mega zero waste project at the Chennai Airport. Her enterprise, TrashCon Labs Private Limited has Shell, Cisco and several corporate giants as partners. She began TrashCon at a humble age of 21 and is currently impacting 30,000 lives every day. She is on a mission to ensure that waste is a history soon so that our kids and future generations can enjoy the earth as we do. Her innovation has impacted lakhs of lives, prevented 22 different types of diseases, 1600 Tons Carbon Emissions Prevented Every day, 146 Tons GHG Emissions Prevented Every day, 54 Acres Landfill Area Saved Everyday.
 
 
 
Mr. Masaki Takao, CEO & Co-Founder, JEPlan

Takao holds a BSc Degree in Chemical Engineering from Tokyo Institute of Technology and studied Technology Management for Innovation at the University of Tokyo. Before founding JEPLAN, he worked with Osaka University. In 2009, Takao founded JEPLAN with Iwamoto and he has been in charge of the whole operation, including technology development, factory operation, finance, and management. He developed the bio-ethanol production technology from cotton in collaboration with a chemical company. He also led a couple of research & development projects, such as ethanol production from waste gas, also the mobile phone recycling process through pyrolysis. Mr. Pei Kang Ng, CEO, TRIA PTE. LTD.

Pei Kang is the CEO of TRIA, an award-winning, sustainable and innovative foodware company. TRIA’s products serve more than 100 million meals a year with clients consisting of brands such as Old Chang Kee, 4Fingers and SATS. In 2019, TRIA achieved a technology breakthrough that allows a closed-loop outcome for single-use foodware. This end-to-end solution, Bio24, could be a game changer for the packaging industry. Pei Kang has a background in R&D, innovation and technology management. He was the former design innovation lead for the global portfolio of Philips TV and Home Theatre System, where he directed R&D activities across Philips Netherlands, Hong Kong, and Singapore. A.K.M. Rafique Ahammed, Director General, Department of Environment

Dr. Ahammed started his career in Bangladesh Civil Service (Administration) Cadre in 1991 and served in a number of field and central positions over the last 27 years.
